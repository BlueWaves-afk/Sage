## Profile
The [[United States]] is a significant player in the global oil supply chain, with a daily export capacity of 4.0 million barrels per day (mbpd). It is not subject to international sanctions, allowing it to engage freely in the global oil market.

## Role
As a major exporter, the [[United States]] plays a crucial role in stabilizing global oil supply, particularly in regions like [[Cape of Good Hope]], where maritime routes are critical for oil transportation. Its exports contribute to the diversification of supply sources for importing countries, enhancing global energy security.

## Risk
The [[United States]] faces risks associated with geopolitical tensions, domestic policy shifts, and environmental regulations that could impact its oil production and export capabilities. Additionally, disruptions in key maritime routes, such as those around the [[Cape of Good Hope]], could affect the delivery of U.S. oil exports to global markets.
