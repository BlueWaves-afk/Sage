## Profile
[[Arab Light]] is a medium-gravity, medium-sour crude oil grade originating from Saudi Arabia. It has an API gravity of 32.8 and a sulfur content of 1.96%. This crude is characterized by a balanced yield structure, producing 22% naphtha, 13% kerosene, 20% diesel, 22% vacuum gas oil (VGO), and 23% residue.

## Role
[[Arab Light]] serves as a pivotal grade within the global crude oil market due to its versatile yield profile. It is particularly valued by refineries capable of maximizing middle distillate outputs, such as [[Jamnagar Refinery]] and [[Vadinar Refinery]] in India. The balanced product slate makes it suitable for complex refineries aiming for optimal naphtha, kerosene, diesel, and VGO production.

## Risk
The primary risk associated with [[Arab Light]] revolves around geopolitical stability in Saudi Arabia, a major producer. Any disruptions in the region could impact supply chains globally. Additionally, refineries like [[Panipat]] that rely heavily on this crude must manage the risk of supply continuity and price volatility inherent in the global oil market.
