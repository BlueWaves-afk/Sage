## Profile
[[Bonny Light]] is a [[NNPC]]n sweet crude oil grade, characterized by an API gravity of 38.0 and a sulfur content of 0.37%. It is extracted from the Niger Delta region, specifically around the Bonny terminal. 

## Role
Bonny Light is highly valued in the global oil market due to its low sulfur content and high API gravity, making it suitable for producing high-quality refined products. Its yield percentages are as follows: naphtha at 26%, kerosene at 14%, diesel at 23%, vacuum gas oil (VGO) at 20%, and residue at 17%. These characteristics make it a preferred feedstock for refineries aiming to maximize production of light-end products.

## Risk
The production and export of Bonny Light are susceptible to geopolitical instability, militant activities, and infrastructure vulnerabilities in [[NNPC]]. These factors can lead to supply disruptions, impacting global oil markets. Additionally, environmental concerns and regulatory changes in importing countries could affect demand.
