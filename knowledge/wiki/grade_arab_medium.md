## Profile
[[Arab Medium]] is a crude oil grade originating from Saudi Arabia, characterized by an API gravity of 30.4 and a sulfur content of 2.59%. This medium-sour crude is processed in various refineries, notably the [[Jamnagar Refinery]] and Mangaluru Refinery in India, to yield a diverse range of petroleum products.

## Role
The primary role of [[Arab Medium]] in the oil supply chain is its versatility in refining. Its product yield includes 19% naphtha, 12% kerosene, 19% diesel, 22% vacuum gas oil (VGO), and 28% residue. These yields make it a valuable feedstock for refineries aiming to produce a balanced slate of refined products.

## Risk
The geopolitical stability of Saudi Arabia directly impacts the supply reliability of [[Arab Medium]]. Any disruptions in the region can affect the crude's availability and pricing, influencing refineries dependent on this grade, such as those in India. Additionally, fluctuations in global oil demand and refining margins can alter the economic viability of processing [[Arab Medium]].
