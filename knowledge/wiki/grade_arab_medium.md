## Profile
[[Arab Medium]] is a crude oil grade originating from Saudi Arabia, characterized by an API gravity of 30.4 and a sulfur content of 2.59%. This medium-sour crude is a significant component in the global oil supply chain, particularly valued for its balanced yield across various refined products.

## Role
The refining of [[Arab Medium]] yields 19% naphtha, 12% kerosene, 19% diesel, 22% vacuum gas oil (VGO), and 28% residue. These yields make it suitable for refineries like [[Jamnagar Refinery]] and Mangaluru Refinery, which are configured to maximize the production of high-value distillates and intermediate products. Its medium-sour profile allows for flexible integration into diverse refining configurations, enhancing its utility in global refining operations.

## Risk
The geopolitical stability of Saudi Arabia directly influences the supply reliability of [[Arab Medium]]. Any disruptions in the region can impact global supply chains, affecting refineries dependent on this crude grade. Additionally, fluctuations in global sulfur cap regulations pose a risk to the demand for medium-sour crudes, potentially altering market dynamics for [[Arab Medium]].
