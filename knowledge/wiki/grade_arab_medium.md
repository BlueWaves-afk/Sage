## Profile
Arab Medium is a crude oil grade originating from Saudi Arabia, characterized by an API gravity of 30.4 and a sulfur content of 2.59%. This medium-sour crude is a significant component of the global oil supply chain, reflecting the strategic importance of Saudi Arabia in global energy markets.

## Role
Arab Medium serves as a feedstock for various refinery configurations, yielding 19% naphtha, 12% kerosene, 19% diesel, 22% vacuum gas oil (VGO), and 28% residue. Its versatility allows it to be processed in complex refineries such as the [[Jamnagar Refinery]], optimizing product slates for high-value outputs. Additionally, it is utilized in coastal refineries like [[Mangaluru]], contributing to regional energy security and supply chain stability.

## Risk
The geopolitical stability of Saudi Arabia directly impacts the supply of Arab Medium. Any disruptions in the region can lead to supply chain vulnerabilities, affecting refineries dependent on this crude grade. Additionally, fluctuations in global oil prices and shifts in refinery demand patterns pose risks to the consistent utilization of Arab Medium in the global market.
