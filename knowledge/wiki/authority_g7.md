## Profile
The G7 Price Cap Coalition is an initiative by the [[G7 Price Cap Coalition]] nations aimed at limiting the revenue Russia can generate from its oil exports. This coalition seeks to impose a maximum price on Russian oil, thereby reducing Russia's financial capacity to fund its military operations while ensuring a stable global oil supply.

## Role
The primary role of the G7 Price Cap Coalition is to implement and enforce a price ceiling on Russian oil. This involves coordinating among member nations to ensure compliance by oil traders, insurers, and shipping companies. The coalition also monitors market dynamics to adjust the price cap as necessary, aiming to balance market stability with the geopolitical objective of constraining Russia's economic power.

## Risk
The implementation of the price cap poses several risks, including potential retaliation from Russia, such as supply disruptions or increased alignment with non-G7 nations. There is also the risk of market volatility, as the price cap could lead to uncertainties in oil pricing and supply chains. Additionally, the effectiveness of the cap depends on widespread adoption and enforcement, which may face challenges due to varying levels of commitment among global actors.
