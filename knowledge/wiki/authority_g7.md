## Profile
The G7 Price Cap Coalition is an initiative led by the [[G7 Price Cap Coalition]] nations aimed at limiting the revenue Russia can generate from its oil exports. The coalition seeks to impose a maximum price on Russian oil, thereby reducing Russia's financial capacity to fund its military operations while ensuring a stable global oil supply.

## Role
The primary role of the G7 Price Cap Coalition is to implement and enforce the price cap on Russian oil. This involves coordinating among member nations to ensure compliance with the cap, leveraging their collective economic influence to pressure other countries and entities to adhere to the imposed limits. The coalition also monitors the effectiveness of the price cap and adjusts strategies as necessary to maintain its impact.

## Risk
The main risks associated with the G7 Price Cap Coalition include potential retaliation from Russia, such as supply disruptions or rerouting oil to non-compliant markets. Additionally, there is a risk of circumvention by Russia through shadow shipping or alternative payment mechanisms. The coalition must also navigate the complexities of global oil markets and the varying degrees of commitment from non-G7 nations.
