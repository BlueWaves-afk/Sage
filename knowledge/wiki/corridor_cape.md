## Location and Significance
The Cape of Good Hope is a rocky headland on the Atlantic coast of the Cape Peninsula in South Africa, located at coordinates -34.36 latitude and 18.47 longitude. Although commonly misconceived as the southern tip of Africa, the actual southernmost point is Cape Agulhas, approximately 150 kilometers to the east-southeast. The Cape of Good Hope marks the point where a ship traveling along the African coastline begins to move more eastward than southward, making it a critical waypoint on the Cape Route.

## Oil Supply Chain Throughput
The Cape of Good Hope corridor facilitates a significant throughput in the oil supply chain, with a recorded throughput of 7.0 million barrels per day (mbpd). This strategic location ensures the continuous flow of oil from various producing regions to global markets.

The Cape of Good Hope is classified as a choke point with a severity index of 0.15. This relatively low severity indicates that while the Cape is a critical passage, the impact of any disruption would be moderate compared to more severe choke points in the global oil supply chain.
