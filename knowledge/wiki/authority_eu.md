## Sanctions Overview

The [[EU]] (EU) employs sanctions as a critical component of its Common Foreign and Security Policy (CFSP). These measures aim to influence the behavior or policies of targeted entities, including governments, companies, groups, organizations, and individuals.

## Targeted Parties

### Countries/Governments
- **Afghanistan** (2021): Sanctions against the Taliban government.
- **Belarus** (2005): Sanctions against those affiliated with the Alexander Lukashenko government.
- **Burundi** (2015): Sanctions against government officials undermining democracy.
- **China** (2021): Sanctions over alleged human rights abuses on the Uyghurs in Xinjiang.
- **Iran** (2011): Sanctions related to military support of Russia's war against Ukraine and human rights violations.
- **Mali** (2021): Sanctions against officials involved in the 2021 coup d'état.
- **Myanmar** (2021): Sanctions against the Burmese Junta following the 2021 coup d'état.
- **Nicaragua** (2019): Sanctions against the Daniel Ortega government for deteriorating democracy and human rights.
- **Niger** (2023): Sanctions over the 2023 coup d'état.
- **North Korea** (2016): Sanctions due to its nuclear program and human rights situation.
- **Russia** (2014): Sanctions over violating Ukraine's territorial integrity.
- **Transnistria** (2003): Sanctions for undermining Moldova's territorial integrity.
- **Crimea, Donetsk People's Republic, Luhansk People's Republic** (2014): Sanctions for undermining Ukraine's territorial integrity.
- **Venezuela** (2017): Sanctions against the Nicolas Maduro government for deteriorating democracy and human rights.
- **Zimbabwe** (2002): Sanctions against the ZANU-PF government for deteriorating democracy and human rights.

### Other Sanctions
- **Bosnia and Herzegovina** (2011): Framework for imposing penalties on individuals endangering the country's security and integrity.
- **Central African Republic** (2013): Sanctions on individuals and entities threatening peace and stability.
- **Democratic Republic of the Congo** (1993): Sanctions on individuals and entities threatening peace and stability.
- **Guatemala** (2024): Sanctions in response to attempts to nullify democratic election results.
- **Guinea** (2009): Sanctions on individuals and entities threatening peace and stability.
- **Guinea-Bissau** (2012): Sanctions targeting those undermining stability and the rule of law.
- **Haiti** (2023): Sanctions on individuals and entities threatening peace and stability.
- **Iraq** (2003): Specific restrictions on trade in cultural heritage goods and asset freeze on former President Saddam Hussein and his regime.
- **Lebanon** (2006): Sanctions based on UN resolutions targeting individuals threatening Lebanon's stability.
- **Libya** (2011): Sanctions related to serious human rights abuses.
- **Serbia** (1994): Limited to a ban on settling specific claims related to revoked UN regulations.
- **Somalia** (1992): Sanctions in view of the situation in Somalia.
- **South Sudan** (2016): Sanctions in view of the situation in South Sudan.
- **Sudan** (1994): Sanctions in view of the situation in Sudan.
- **Tunisia** (2011): Sanctions against those misusing state funds and undermining democracy.
- **Turkey** (2019): Sanctions against state-owned Turkish hydrocarbon companies.
