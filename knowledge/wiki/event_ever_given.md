## Event Summary
The [[Ever Given Suez Blockage]], a 400-metre-long container ship carrying approximately 20,000 TEU, grounded and blocked the Suez Canal on 23 March 2021. The blockage lasted six days until the ship was dislodged on 29 March 2021.

## Impact on Shipping
Over 300 vessels were obstructed at both ends of the canal. By 28 March, at least 369 ships were queuing, including bulk carriers, container ships, and crude oil tankers, representing approximately 16.9 million tonnes of cargo.

## Economic Consequences
Lloyd's List estimated that the delayed goods were valued at approximately $400 million per hour, equating to ~$9.6 billion per day. The Suez Canal Authority lost an estimated $15 million daily in transit fees during the closure. Oil prices initially rose due to strong winds and market psychology but fell after the canal reopened on 29 March as the delayed oil supply resumed.

## Supply Chain Resilience
The incident highlighted vulnerabilities in supply-chain resilience, further exacerbated by existing COVID-19-era disruptions.
