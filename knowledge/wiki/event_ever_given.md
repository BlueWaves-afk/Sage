## Event Summary
- The [[Ever Given Suez Blockage]] container ship, carrying approximately 20,000 TEU, grounded and blocked the Suez Canal on 23 March 2021.
- The blockage lasted six days, until the ship was dislodged on 29 March 2021.

## Impact on Shipping
- Over 300 vessels were obstructed at both ends of the canal.
- By 28 March, at least 369 ships were queuing, including bulk carriers, container ships, and crude oil tankers.
- The total cargo delayed amounted to approximately 16.9 million tonnes.

## Economic Consequences
- Lloyd's List estimated the value of delayed goods at ~$400 million per hour, equating to ~$9.6 billion per day.
- The Suez Canal Authority lost an estimated $15 million daily in transit fees during the closure.
- Oil prices initially rose due to strong winds and market psychology but fell after the canal reopened as delayed oil supply resumed.

## Supply Chain Vulnerabilities
- The incident highlighted vulnerabilities in global supply chain resilience.
- It compounded existing disruptions caused by the COVID-19 pandemic.
