## Event Overview
The "2025 Iran-Israel Conflict" refers to a series of direct military strikes between Iran and Israel that began on June 1, 2025. These strikes significantly raised the risk of the closure of the Strait of Hormuz, a critical chokepoint for global oil shipments.

## Severity and Impact
The severity of the conflict is rated at 0.9, indicating a high level of escalation and potential for widespread impact on global oil supply chains. The closure or threat of closure of the Strait of Hormuz poses a severe risk to the continuous flow of oil from major producers in the Persian Gulf.

## Contextual Background
The conflict is part of the broader Iran–Israel conflict, which has transitioned from proxy hostilities to direct military confrontations since 2024. The 2025 conflict is specifically noted as the "Twelve-Day War," characterized by intense military exchanges between the two nations.
