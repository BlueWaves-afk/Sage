## Profile
The [[Urals]] crude grade, originating from Russia, is characterized by an API gravity of 31.3 and a sulfur content of 1.30%. This medium-sour crude is a blend primarily from fields in the Volga-Urals region. 

## Role
Urals crude is pivotal in the European refining market, known for its balanced yield structure. It produces 19% naphtha, 12% kerosene, 20% diesel, 22% vacuum gas oil (VGO), and 27% residue. These yields make it suitable for complex refineries like the [[Jamnagar Refinery]], capable of maximizing value from its diverse output.

## Risk
Geopolitical tensions involving Russia pose significant risks to the supply chain of Urals crude. Sanctions and diplomatic conflicts can disrupt exports, affecting refineries dependent on this grade. Additionally, environmental regulations targeting sulfur content may necessitate further processing, increasing operational costs.
