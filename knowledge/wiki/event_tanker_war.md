## Event Overview
The Tanker War was a series of attacks on merchant vessels in the Persian Gulf and [[Strait of Hormuz]] from 1981 to 1988, as part of the Iran–Iraq War. Iraq conducted 283 attacks, while Iran carried out 168. Iraq initially targeted ships bound for Iranian ports in 1981, expanding attacks to the Kharg Island oil-loading complex in March 1984. Iran responded with retaliatory attacks, turning it into a two-way conflict.

## Strategic Objectives and Impact
Iraq aimed to provoke Iran into closing the [[Strait of Hormuz]], hoping to trigger US intervention. Kuwait and Saudi Arabia supported Iraq. Over 30 million tonnes of cargo were damaged, and more than 100 sailors were killed between 1981 and 1987. The US intervened in 1986 to protect Kuwaiti tankers and reflagged Kuwaiti vessels in 1987 under Operation Earnest Will (July 1987–September 1988). Iran employed speedboat attacks and Chinese-made Silkworm missiles. The Tanker War concluded on 4 August 1988, aligning with the end of the broader Iran–Iraq War.
