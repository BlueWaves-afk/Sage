## GeoEvent Overview

The [[2024 Red Sea Crisis]] began on 19 October 2023, driven by the Houthi movement's missile and drone attacks on Red Sea shipping. The Houthis, an Iran-backed militant organization controlling parts of Yemen, targeted Israel-linked ships and vessels of non-aligned nations in the Bab-el-Mandeb strait, the southern gateway to the Suez Canal. The crisis was initiated in response to the October 2023 Gaza war, with the Houthis demanding a ceasefire in Gaza.

## Impact on Shipping

The Houthi attacks led to the rerouting of hundreds of commercial vessels around the Cape of Good Hope, avoiding the Bab-el-Mandeb strait and the Suez Canal. The Armed Conflict Location and Event Data project reported 178 vessels attacked over a two-year period, resulting in the sinking of four ships and the deaths of nine sailors.

## International Response

In January 2024, the UN Security Council adopted Resolution 2722, affirming freedom of navigation in the Red Sea. The US-led Operation Prosperity Guardian, with UK participation, conducted strikes against Houthi positions. A bilateral US–Houthi ceasefire took effect on 6 May 2025, following the implementation of the Gaza peace plan on 10 October 2025. However, the Houthis threatened to escalate again on 28 February 2026 amid the broader Iran–US–Israel conflict.

## Severity and Duration

The crisis severity is rated at 0.8, indicating significant disruption to global trade and energy supply chains. The event began on 1 January 2024 and continued through the reported period, with intermittent escalations and de-escalations.
