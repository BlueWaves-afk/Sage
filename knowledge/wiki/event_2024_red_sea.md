## Event Overview
The [[2024 Red Sea Crisis]] was initiated by the Houthi movement, an Iran-backed militant organization controlling parts of Yemen. The crisis began in response to the October 2023 Gaza war. Starting 19 October 2023, the Houthis launched missiles and armed drones targeting Israel and merchant vessels in the Red Sea, demanding a ceasefire in Gaza. The Bab-el-Mandeb strait, the southern maritime gateway to the Suez Canal, became the primary attack zone. The Houthis declared any Israel-linked ship a target but also struck vessels of non-aligned nations.

## Impact on Shipping
The Houthi attacks led to the rerouting of hundreds of commercial vessels around the Cape of Good Hope in southern Africa, avoiding both the Bab-el-Mandeb strait and the Suez Canal. According to the Armed Conflict Location and Event Data project, the Houthis attacked 178 vessels over a two-year blockade, sinking four ships and killing nine sailors.

## International Response
The UN Security Council adopted Resolution 2722 in January 2024, affirming freedom of navigation. The US-led Operation Prosperity Guardian, with the UK joining, struck Houthi positions in response to the attacks. A bilateral US–Houthi ceasefire took effect on 6 May 2025. Following the implementation of the Gaza peace plan on 10 October 2025, the Houthis halted attacks but threatened escalation again on 28 February 2026 amid the broader Iran–US–Israel conflict.
