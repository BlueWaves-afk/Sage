## GeoEvent Overview
The [[2024 Red Sea Crisis]] was initiated by the Houthi movement, an Iran-backed militant organization controlling parts of Yemen. The crisis began in response to the October 2023 Gaza war, with the Houthis launching missiles and armed drones at Israel and merchant vessels in the Red Sea, demanding a ceasefire in Gaza.

## Attack Details
The primary attack zone was the Bab-el-Mandeb strait, the southern maritime gateway to the Suez Canal. The Houthis declared any Israel-linked ship a target but also struck vessels of non-aligned nations. Over a two-year blockade, they attacked 178 vessels, sinking four ships and killing nine sailors.

## Impact on Trade
In response to the attacks, hundreds of commercial vessels were rerouted around the Cape of Good Hope (southern Africa), avoiding both Bab-el-Mandeb and the Suez Canal. This diversion significantly impacted global trade routes and supply chains.

## International Response
The UN Security Council adopted Resolution 2722 in January 2024, affirming freedom of navigation. The US-led Operation Prosperity Guardian, with UK participation, struck Houthi positions. A bilateral US–Houthi ceasefire took effect on 6 May 2025. The crisis saw a temporary halt following the Gaza peace plan's implementation on 10 October 2025, but the Houthis threatened escalation again on 28 February 2026 amid the broader Iran–US–Israel conflict.
