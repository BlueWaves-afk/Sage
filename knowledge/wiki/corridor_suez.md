## Geopolitical Significance
The [[Suez Canal]] is a critical maritime corridor connecting the Mediterranean Sea to the Red Sea, facilitating a direct route between Europe and Asia. Its strategic importance is underscored by its role as a naval shortcut and choke point, particularly for nations with coastlines and bases on both the Mediterranean and the Red Sea, such as Egypt and Israel.

## Operational Characteristics
- **Throughput**: The canal handles approximately 4.9 million barrels per day (mbpd) of oil.
- **Choke Severity**: Rated at 0.60, indicating a significant but not extreme vulnerability in the global oil supply chain.
- **Location**: Situated at coordinates 30.7°N latitude and 32.3°E longitude.
- The canal has a history of incidents, including the 2021 obstruction and the 2023 grounding, which highlight its operational challenges and the potential for disruptions.
