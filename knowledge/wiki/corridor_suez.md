## Geopolitical Significance
The [[Suez Canal]] is a critical maritime corridor connecting the Mediterranean Sea to the Red Sea, facilitating a direct route between Europe and Asia. Its strategic location makes it a vital choke point in global oil supply chains, with a choke severity index of 0.60. The canal's throughput capacity is approximately 4.9 million barrels per day (mbpd), underscoring its importance in energy security.

## Operational Characteristics
The [[Suez Canal]] operates as a sea-level waterway without locks, allowing free flow of seawater. It extends 193.3 km (120.1 mi) from Port Said in the north to Suez Port in the south. The canal's latitude and longitude coordinates are 30.70500°N and 32.34417°E, respectively.
