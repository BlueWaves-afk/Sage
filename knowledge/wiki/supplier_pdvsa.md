## Profile
- **Canonical Name**: Petróleos de Venezuela, S.A. (PDVSA)
- **Country**: Venezuela
- **Primary Activity**: State-owned oil and gas company responsible for the exploration, production, refining, and export of petroleum and petroleum products.

## Role
- **Export Volume**: Approximately 0.7 million barrels per day (mbpd).
- **Global Supply Chain**: Key player in the Latin American oil market, with significant exports to various international markets, including the United States (pre-sanctions), China, and countries in the Caribbean.
- **Refining**: Operates several refineries within Venezuela and has joint ventures with foreign companies for refining operations abroad.

## Risk
- **Sanctions**: Subject to international sanctions, primarily by the United States, restricting its ability to trade freely in global markets.
- **Operational Challenges**: Faces significant operational difficulties due to economic crises, lack of investment, and brain drain, impacting production and export capabilities.
- **Geopolitical Risk**: High political risk due to the unstable domestic political environment and international relations, affecting supply reliability.
- **Navigational Risk**: Exports often transit through critical chokepoints such as the [[Cape of Good Hope]], exposing shipments to potential disruptions.
