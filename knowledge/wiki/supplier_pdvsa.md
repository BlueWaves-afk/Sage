## Profile
Petróleos de Venezuela, S.A. (PDVSA) is the state-owned oil company of [[PDVSA]]. It plays a critical role in the nation's economy, contributing significantly to its GDP and public finances. PDVSA operates throughout the oil supply chain, from exploration and production to refining and marketing.

## Role
PDVSA is a key supplier in the global oil market, with a daily export capacity of approximately 0.7 million barrels per day (mbpd). The company's operations extend to various international markets, though its primary export routes often navigate through critical chokepoints such as the [[Cape of Good Hope]].

## Risk
PDVSA faces substantial operational risks due to international sanctions imposed on [[PDVSA]]. These sanctions complicate its ability to trade freely in the global market, impacting its export capabilities and financial stability. The geopolitical climate further exacerbates these challenges, making PDVSA a high-risk entity in the oil supply chain.
