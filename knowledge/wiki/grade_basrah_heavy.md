## Profile
[[Basrah Heavy]] is a crude oil grade originating from Iraq. It is characterized by an API gravity of 24.0, classifying it as a heavy crude, and a high sulfur content of 4.05%, making it a sour crude. 

## Role
[[Basrah Heavy]] is primarily refined into heavier products due to its high residue yield of 40%. Its refining process yields 13% naphtha, 10% kerosene, 16% diesel, and 21% vacuum gas oil (VGO). Refineries with complex configurations, such as [[Jamnagar Refinery]] and [[Paradip]], are well-suited to process this grade, maximizing the output of valuable middle distillates and residues.

## Risk
The high sulfur content of [[Basrah Heavy]] poses environmental and operational risks, requiring refineries to have adequate desulfurization capabilities to meet regulatory standards. Geopolitical instability in Iraq also presents supply chain risks, potentially affecting the consistent flow of this crude grade to global markets.
