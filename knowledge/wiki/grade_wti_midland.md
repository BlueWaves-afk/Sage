## Profile
[[WTI Midland]] is a light sweet crude oil grade originating from the Permian Basin in the United States. It has an API gravity of 40.0 and a sulfur content of 0.40%, categorizing it as a light sweet crude. 

## Role
WTI Midland is primarily refined into high-value products due to its favorable characteristics. Its yield includes 30% naphtha, 16% kerosene, 22% diesel, 18% vacuum gas oil (VGO), and 14% residue. Refineries such as [[Jamnagar Refinery]] value WTI Midland for its high naphtha and diesel yields, which are crucial for producing gasoline and other high-demand petroleum products.

## Risk
The primary risks associated with WTI Midland include fluctuations in U.S. shale production and transportation constraints within the Permian Basin. Geopolitical stability in the United States and the efficiency of pipeline infrastructure directly impact the supply and pricing of WTI Midland.
