## Profile
[[WTI Midland]] is a light sweet crude oil grade originating from the Permian Basin in the United States. It has an API gravity of 40.0 and a sulfur content of 0.40%, categorizing it as a light sweet crude. 

## Role
[[WTI Midland]] is integral to the U.S. oil supply chain, particularly valued for its high yield of valuable products. Its refining process yields 30% naphtha, 16% kerosene, 22% diesel, 18% vacuum gas oil (VGO), and 14% residue. These characteristics make it a preferred feedstock for complex refineries like the [[Jamnagar Refinery]], which can maximize the production of high-value distillates.

## Risk
The primary risks associated with [[WTI Midland]] include fluctuations in U.S. production levels and transportation constraints within the Permian Basin. Geopolitical stability in the region and the efficiency of pipeline infrastructure are critical factors influencing the consistent supply and pricing of [[WTI Midland]].
