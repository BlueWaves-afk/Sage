## Profile
[[Basrah Medium]] is a crude oil grade originating from Iraq. It has an API gravity of 27.9, classifying it as a medium crude, and a high sulfur content of 3.00%, making it a sour crude. 

## Role
[[Basrah Medium]] is significant in the global oil supply chain due to its substantial yield characteristics. It produces 17% naphtha, 11% kerosene, 18% diesel, 21% vacuum gas oil (VGO), and 33% residue. These yields make it valuable for refineries capable of handling high-sulfur crude, particularly those in regions like [[Mangaluru]], where complex refining capabilities exist.

## Risk
The primary risks associated with [[Basrah Medium]] include geopolitical instability in Iraq, which can disrupt supply. Additionally, its high sulfur content necessitates refineries with desulfurization capabilities, limiting its refinery options and potentially exposing it to higher processing costs.
