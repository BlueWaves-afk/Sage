## Profile
[[Basrah Medium]] is a crude oil grade originating from Iraq. It has an API gravity of 27.9, categorizing it as a medium-heavy crude, and a high sulfur content of 3.00%, making it a sour crude. 

## Role
[[Basrah Medium]] is primarily refined for its substantial yield of residue (33%), which is valuable for producing heavy fuel oils and bitumen. It also yields significant percentages of naphtha (17%), kerosene (11%), diesel (18%), and vacuum gas oil (VGO) (21%). These products make it a versatile feedstock for refineries capable of handling sour crudes.

## Risk
The geopolitical instability in Iraq poses a significant risk to the consistent supply of [[Basrah Medium]]. Additionally, its high sulfur content requires refineries to have appropriate desulphurization capabilities, limiting the pool of potential processors. The crude’s transportation, often through critical chokepoints such as the [[Mangaluru]] port, adds another layer of supply chain vulnerability.
