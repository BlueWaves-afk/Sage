## Event Overview
In June 2019, two oil tankers, the Kokuka Courageous and Front Altair, were attacked near the Strait of Hormuz in the Gulf of Oman. Both vessels, carrying petroleum products from Gulf producers to Asian markets, sustained fire damage. US officials attributed the attacks to limpet mines and accused Iran of involvement, a claim Iran denied.

## Geopolitical Impact
The incident heightened tensions in the region, leading to a temporary 4% rise in oil prices due to supply uncertainty, which later settled around a 2% increase. Additionally, war-risk insurance premiums for supertankers in the Persian Gulf surged from $50,000 to $185,000 per voyage, increasing shipping and fuel costs.
