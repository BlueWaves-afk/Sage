## Event Overview
On 13 June 2019, two oil tankers, the Kokuka Courageous and Front Altair, were attacked in international waters near the Strait of Hormuz in the Gulf of Oman. Both vessels sustained fire damage from what US officials alleged were limpet mine attacks. The Kokuka Courageous was Panama-flagged and Japan-operated, while the Front Altair was Marshall Islands-flagged and Norway-operated. Both tankers were carrying petroleum products from Gulf producers to Asian markets.

## Attribution and Denial
US officials attributed the attacks to Iran, releasing footage they claimed showed Iranian Revolutionary Guard forces removing an unexploded mine from one of the vessels. The US was supported in its claims by Saudi Arabia, the UK, and Germany. Iran denied any involvement, dismissing the allegations as suspicious.

## Market Impact
The attacks led to an initial 4% rise in oil prices, which later settled approximately 2% higher due to supply uncertainty. Additionally, war-risk insurance premiums for supertankers in the Persian Gulf increased significantly, from $50,000 to $185,000 per voyage, thereby raising shipping and fuel costs.
