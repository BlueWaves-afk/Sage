## Profile
[[Kuwait Export Crude]] is a medium-gravity, high-sulfur crude oil with an API gravity of 31.0 and a sulfur content of 2.55%. Originating from [[Kuwait Petroleum Corporation]], it is characterized by a diverse yield profile, including 18% naphtha, 12% kerosene, 19% diesel, 21% vacuum gas oil (VGO), and 30% residue.

## Role
[[Kuwait Export Crude]] serves as a vital feedstock for refineries capable of handling high-sulfur crudes. Its yield profile makes it particularly valuable for producing middle distillates and heavy products. Refineries such as [[Jamnagar Refinery]] leverage its properties to maximize diesel and VGO output, crucial for meeting global energy demands.

## Risk
The high sulfur content of [[Kuwait Export Crude]] poses environmental and operational risks, necessitating desulfurization processes to meet regulatory standards. Geopolitical tensions in the Persian Gulf could disrupt supply chains, impacting refineries reliant on this crude. Additionally, fluctuations in global oil prices and shifts in refinery configurations pose economic risks to its market value.
