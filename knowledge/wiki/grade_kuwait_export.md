## Profile
[[Kuwait Export Crude]] is a medium-gravity, high-sulfur crude oil with an API gravity of 31.0 and a sulfur content of 2.55%. Originating from [[Kuwait Petroleum Corporation]], this crude is characterized by its balanced yield structure, producing 18% naphtha, 12% kerosene, 19% diesel, 21% vacuum gas oil (VGO), and 30% residue.

## Role
[[Kuwait Export Crude]] serves as a vital feedstock for refineries capable of handling high-sulfur crudes. Its yield profile makes it suitable for producing a range of refined products, including significant volumes of middle distillates and residues. Refineries like [[Jamnagar Refinery]], with complex configurations, can efficiently process this crude to maximize product output.

## Risk
The high sulfur content of [[Kuwait Export Crude]] poses environmental and operational risks, necessitating desulfurization processes to meet regulatory standards. Geopolitical stability in [[Kuwait Petroleum Corporation]] is critical, as any disruption in supply could impact global oil markets due to the crude's specific yield characteristics and the reliance of certain refineries on this feedstock.
