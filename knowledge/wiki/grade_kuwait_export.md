## Profile
[[Kuwait Export Crude]] is a medium-heavy crude oil with an API gravity of 31.0 and a sulfur content of 2.55%. Originating from [[Kuwait Petroleum Corporation]], it is characterized by a balanced yield structure: 18% naphtha, 12% kerosene, 19% diesel, 21% vacuum gas oil (VGO), and 30% residue.

## Role
[[Kuwait Export Crude]] serves as a vital feedstock for complex refineries capable of handling higher sulfur content and maximizing middle distillate yields. Notably, it is processed at the [[Jamnagar Refinery]], one of the world's largest, which leverages its advanced configuration to optimize product slates from this crude.

## Risk
The geopolitical stability of [[Kuwait Petroleum Corporation]] directly influences the supply reliability of [[Kuwait Export Crude]]. Any regional instability could impact export volumes. Additionally, its higher sulfur content necessitates refineries with adequate desulfurization capabilities, limiting its viable processing locations to those with suitable infrastructure.
