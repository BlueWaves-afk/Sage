## Role
- **Market Position:** Tupi crude is a medium-heavy, low-sulfur oil primarily produced from the Pre-Salt Layer in the Santos Basin off the coast of Brazil. Its balanced yield profile makes it suitable for refineries configured to produce a mix of light and middle distillates, including naphtha, kerosene, and diesel.
- **Refining Compatibility:** The crude’s properties allow it to be processed efficiently in modern refineries, contributing to a diverse output of petroleum products.

## Risk
- **Geopolitical Factors:** Production and export of Tupi crude are subject to the political and economic stability of Brazil. Changes in government policy, regulatory environment, or economic conditions could impact supply.
- **Operational Risks:** Extraction from the Pre-Salt Layer involves complex and costly deep-water drilling operations, which are susceptible to technical challenges and environmental risks.
- **Market Risks:** Fluctuations in global oil prices and demand for specific petroleum products can affect the profitability and desirability of Tupi crude in the international market.
