 The Iran–Israel conflict is a long-standing geopolitical and military confrontation between the Islamic Republic of Iran and the State of Israel , involving proxy hostilities since 1985 and direct clashes since 2024. &#91; 1 &#93; &#91; 2 &#93; 

 Background 
 Main article: Iranian Revolution 
 Iran and Israel became adversaries following the Iranian Revolution in 1979, when Iran severed ties on 18 February 1979 and adopted an openly hostile stance toward Israel. Over the following decades, the rivalry expanded through proxy forces and regional influence campaigns, including Iran's support for Hezbollah and Hamas , as well as its nuclear program and ballistic missile development. &#91; 3 &#93; &#91; 4 &#93; 
 
 Proxy conflict (1985–present) 
 Main article: Iran–Israel proxy conflict 
 The conflict involved indirect clashes between Israel and Iran-backed forces in Syria , Lebanon and Gaza Strip , and Israeli strikes targeting Iranian positions. &#91; 1 &#93; &#91; 2 &#93; 
 
 Direct conflict (2024–present) 
 2024 conflict 
 Main article: 2024 Iran–Israel conflict 
 Direct confrontations included missile and drone exchanges, with Israeli bombings of Iranian targets in Syria and Iranian retaliatory attacks on Israeli territory. &#91; 5 &#93; 
 
 2025 Twelve-Day War 
 Main article: Twelve-Day War 
 In June 2025, a brief war known as the Twelve-Day War involved Israeli strikes on Iranian military and nuclear facilities and a ceasefire brokered afterwards. &#91; 5 &#93; 
 
 2026 Iran war 
 Main article: 2026 Iran war 
 In late February 2026, after the breakdown of US–Iran negotiations , Israel and the United States conducted coordinated military operations against Iranian targets. Iran retaliated with missile and drone strikes on Israeli territory , causing civilian casualties and infrastructure damage. &#91; 6 &#93; &#91; 7 &#93;
