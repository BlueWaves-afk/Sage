_Wikipedia Tanker War_

The Tanker War was a series of attacks on merchant vessels in the Persian Gulf and Strait of Hormuz from 1981 to 1988, part of the Iran–Iraq War. Iraq was responsible for 283 attacks and Iran for 168. Iraq began targeting ships bound for Iranian ports in 1981; in March 1984 it expanded attacks to the Kharg Island oil-loading complex, after which Iran began retaliatory attacks, making it a two-way conflict.

Iraq's strategy aimed to provoke Iran into closing the Strait of Hormuz, which would trigger US intervention. Kuwait and Saudi Arabia backed Iraq. Over 30 million tonnes of cargo were damaged between 1981 and 1987, and well over 100 sailors were killed. The US intervened in 1986 to protect Kuwaiti tankers; in 1987 Kuwait requested US reflagging of its vessels, leading to Operation Earnest Will (July 1987–September 1988). Iran used speedboat attacks and Chinese-made Silkworm missiles. The Tanker War ended on 4 August 1988 with the broader Iran–Iraq War.
