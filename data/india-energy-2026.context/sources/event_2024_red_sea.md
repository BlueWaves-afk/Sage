## Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Sea_crisis
_Wikipedia Red Sea crisis_

The Houthis, an Iran-backed militant organization controlling parts of Yemen, initiated the crisis in response to the October 2023 Gaza war. Beginning 19 October 2023, the Houthis launched missiles and armed drones at Israel and at merchant vessels in the Red Sea, demanding a ceasefire in Gaza. The Bab-el-Mandeb strait — the southern maritime gateway to the Suez Canal — became the primary attack zone; the Houthis declared any Israel-linked ship a target but also struck vessels of non-aligned nations.

Per the Armed Conflict Location and Event Data project, the Houthis attacked 178 vessels over a two-year blockade, sinking four ships and killing nine sailors. In response, hundreds of commercial vessels were rerouted around the Cape of Good Hope (southern Africa), avoiding both Bab-el-Mandeb and the Suez Canal. The UN Security Council adopted Resolution 2722 in January 2024 affirming freedom of navigation; the US-led Operation Prosperity Guardian struck Houthi positions, with the UK joining.

A bilateral US–Houthi ceasefire took effect 6 May 2025. Following the Gaza peace plan's 10 October 2025 implementation, the Houthis halted attacks; they threatened escalation again on 28 February 2026 amid the broader Iran–US–Israel conflict.
