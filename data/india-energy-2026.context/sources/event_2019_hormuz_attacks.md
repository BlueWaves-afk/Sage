## Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/June_2019_Gulf_of_Oman_incident
_Wikipedia June 2019 Gulf of Oman incident_

On 13 June 2019, two oil tankers transiting the Gulf of Oman near the Strait of Hormuz were attacked in international waters (25°28′59″N 57°32′35″E). The Kokuka Courageous (Panama-flagged, Japan-operated) and Front Altair (Marshall Islands-flagged, Norway-operated) sustained fire damage; both carried petroleum products from Gulf producers bound for Asian markets.

US officials alleged the vessels were struck with limpet mines and released footage purporting to show Iranian Revolutionary Guard forces removing an unexploded mine. The US blamed Iran (backed by Saudi Arabia, the UK, and Germany); Iran categorically denied involvement, calling the claims suspicious.

Oil prices initially rose 4% before settling ~2% higher on supply uncertainty. War-risk insurance premiums for Persian Gulf supertankers jumped from $50,000 to $185,000 per voyage, raising shipping and fuel costs.
