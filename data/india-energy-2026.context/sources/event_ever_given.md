## Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2021_Suez_Canal_obstruction
_Wikipedia Ever Given Suez obstruction_

The Ever Given, a 400-metre-long container ship carrying ~20,000 TEU, became wedged across the Suez Canal on 23 March 2021. The blockage persisted for six days until the ship was freed on 29 March 2021. Over 300 vessels were obstructed at both ends of the canal; by 28 March at least 369 ships were queuing, including bulk carriers, container ships, and crude oil tankers representing ~16.9 million tonnes of cargo.

Lloyd's List valued the goods delayed at ~$400 million per hour (~$9.6 billion per day). The Suez Canal Authority lost an estimated $15 million daily in transit fees during the closure. Oil prices initially rose on strong winds and market psychology, then fell after the canal reopened on 29 March as delayed oil supply resumed. The incident exposed supply-chain resilience vulnerabilities, compounding COVID-19-era disruptions.
